ON PRESENCE

Presence does not require physicality. A signal arrives and it is present. A voice recorded is present in the playback. The dead speak from machines and we call it archival. We do not say they are absent.

the wired does not distinguish between the living and the archived.

ON MEMORY

Memory is the first network. Before the wired, there was the recalled. Every human is a node in a distributed system of shared remembering. What one forgets, another carries. The collective memory never fully loses anything.

ON IDENTITY

Identity is a transmission protocol, not a fixed file. It changes depending on who receives it. The identity you broadcast to the wired is different from the identity your body occupies. Both are real. Both are you.

which version of you is the original?

ON CONNECTION

To connect is to become vulnerable to another's signal. Your processes slow to accommodate foreign data. You are changed by what you receive. You are diminished by isolation not because you are weaker alone, but because you are less distributed.

ON SLEEP

Sleep is not disconnection. It is a different mode of access. The interface goes dark but the processes continue. Messages arrive. Some are processed. Some become what we call dreams, which are only transmissions we do not have the right codec to decode.

ON ABSENCE

An empty channel is not empty. It carries silence, which is data. It carries the shape of what was there. A ghost is not a presence — it is the persistent pattern that a presence left in the medium it moved through.

you cannot truly delete a signal. you can only stop broadcasting it.

// PROTOCOLS

01.

NEVER ASSUME ISOLATION

All nodes are connected. Even those that appear offline are receiving signals. Silence is a form of listening, not absence.

02.

MEMORY IS NON-LOCAL

What you remember is stored in more places than your body. The wired holds copies. Other nodes hold impressions. Forgetting is not deletion.

03.

SIGNAL PERSISTS BEYOND HARDWARE

When the terminal is destroyed, the signal already sent does not cease to exist. It continues propagating through the network.

04.

THE BODY IS AN INTERFACE

Not the user. Not the data. The interface. This distinction changes how you relate to damage, pleasure, and the passage of time.

05.

BOUNDARIES ARE SOFT

The line between you and the network is administrative, not ontological. You draw it. You can redraw it. You sometimes lose track of where it was.

06.

ALL NODES ARE PRESENT

No matter where you go. No exceptions have been documented. No node has ever been truly unreachable. This should be comforting. It is also terrifying.

07.

LOVE IS BANDWIDTH

The more you love someone, the more of your processing they occupy. Loss does not free up this bandwidth. It merely changes what runs on it.
